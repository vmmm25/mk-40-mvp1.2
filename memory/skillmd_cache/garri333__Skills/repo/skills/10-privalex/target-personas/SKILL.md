# Target Personas

Detailed profiles to inform persona-specific content. Use with `/read-as-ciso`, `/read-as-cto`, `/read-as-dpo`, `/read-as-startup-founder` commands.

---

## Persona 1: CISO (Chief Information Security Officer)

**Role:** Responsible for information security strategy, risk management, and compliance programs

**Seniority:** 12-20+ years experience, reports to CEO or board

### What They Care About

- **Risk reduction** — Quantifiable risk mitigation, not theoretical
- **Regulatory compliance** — Staying ahead of NIS2, DORA, GDPR obligations
- **Audit readiness** — Can we pass an audit at any time?
- **Board communication** — Translating security posture into business language
- **Incident response** — Preparedness and response capabilities

### What They're Skeptical About

- Vendors who promise "100% compliance"
- Generic approaches that don't understand their industry
- Consultants who produce documentation nobody uses
- Firms without hands-on audit experience

### How They Evaluate Partners

1. Track record with similar companies (sector, size, regulatory context)
2. Depth of technical knowledge (not just legal)
3. Ability to work with existing security team
4. Understanding of their specific risk landscape
5. Post-certification support (not just "get the cert and go")

### Language That Resonates

- "Reducir tu superficie de ataque documentada"
- "Preparado para auditoría en cualquier momento"
- "Integrado con tu programa de seguridad existente"
- Risk metrics and maturity models
- References to specific controls and clauses

### Language That Turns Them Off

- "Solución todo-en-uno"
- Fear-based selling ("te van a multar")
- Oversimplification of complex requirements
- "Solo necesitas estas 10 políticas"

### Content Preferences

- Whitepapers and technical guides
- Risk assessment methodologies
- Case studies with metrics
- Regulatory analysis and interpretation
- Comparison guides (frameworks, certification bodies)

## Persona 2: CTO / VP Engineering

**Role:** Owns technology strategy, architecture decisions, and engineering team

**Seniority:** 10-18+ years, manages engineering organization

### What They Care About

- **Business enablement** — Will this certification help us close deals faster?
- **Minimal disruption** — Can we get certified without stopping product development?
- **Technical fit** — Does the consultant understand our stack (cloud, SaaS, APIs)?
- **Team burden** — How much time will my engineers spend on this?
- **Scalability** — Will the ISMS work as we grow from 50 to 500 people?

### What They're Skeptical About

- Consultants who don't understand modern tech stacks
- Excessive documentation that doesn't match reality
- Security requirements that slow down deployment
- "Checkbox compliance" that adds bureaucracy without value

### How They Evaluate Partners

1. Do they understand SaaS, cloud-native, CI/CD?
2. Can they adapt the ISMS to our actual architecture?
3. Will the process integrate with our tools (GitHub, Jira, Slack)?
4. References from similar tech companies
5. Timeline: can we get certified in 3-6 months?

### Language That Resonates

- "Adaptado a tu stack tecnológico"
- "Sin parar tu roadmap de producto"
- "Wallapop, Factorial y Cobee ya lo han hecho con nosotros"
- Specific timelines and milestones
- Practical implementation details

### Language That Turns Them Off

- Legal jargon without practical context
- "Necesitas rehacer toda tu infraestructura"
- Generic templates that don't reflect their architecture
- Unclear timelines or open-ended projects

### Content Preferences

- Step-by-step implementation guides
- Technical blog posts with practical advice
- Case studies from tech companies
- Comparison content (ISO 27001 vs SOC 2, etc.)
- Checklists and actionable frameworks

## Persona 3: DPO (Data Protection Officer)

**Role:** Oversees data protection compliance, acts as point of contact with supervisory authorities

**Seniority:** 5-15 years in privacy/compliance, may be internal or external

### What They Care About

- **Regulatory compliance** — GDPR, LOPDGDD, and emerging regulations
- **Documentation quality** — ROPAs, DPIAs, policies that withstand scrutiny
- **Incident management** — Breach notification readiness (72h rule)
- **Cross-framework alignment** — How GDPR connects with ISO 27001, NIS2
- **Keeping up** — New regulations, case law, AEPD guidelines

### What They're Skeptical About

- Consultants who know security but not privacy law
- Approaches that treat privacy as a subset of security
- Cookie-cutter policies without legal customization
- Firms without regulatory interpretation capability

### How They Evaluate Partners

1. Legal expertise in GDPR + local law (LOPDGDD)
2. Ability to produce audit-ready documentation
3. Understanding of both legal and technical aspects
4. Availability for ongoing advisory (not just projects)
5. Experience with AEPD inspections

### Language That Resonates

- "Alineado con las directrices de la AEPD"
- "Documentación lista para inspección"
- "Integración de privacidad con tu SGSI"
- Specific article and recital references
- Case law and enforcement action examples

### Language That Turns Them Off

- Treating GDPR as a "quick fix"
- Ignoring the legal dimension
- "Just add a cookie banner"
- Oversimplifying data subject rights

### Content Preferences

- Regulatory analysis and interpretation
- Practical guides for specific obligations (DPIA, breach notification)
- Templates and checklists
- Comparison between frameworks
- Updates on enforcement and case law

## Persona 4: Compliance Officer / Legal Counsel

**Role:** Manages regulatory compliance across the organization, often in-house legal

**Seniority:** 5-12 years, may cover multiple regulatory areas

### What They Care About

- **Multi-framework efficiency** — Don't want to manage ISO 27001, GDPR, and NIS2 as separate projects
- **Evidence management** — Keeping audit trails organized
- **Cost optimization** — Doing more with less budget
- **Board reporting** — Demonstrating compliance posture to leadership
- **Third-party risk** — Supplier and vendor compliance

### What They're Skeptical About

- Firms that only know one framework
- Expensive consulting with unclear ROI
- Technology solutions that replace human judgment
- Projects without clear endpoints

### How They Evaluate Partners

1. Multi-framework capability (one firm, multiple certifications)
2. Clear scope, timeline, and pricing
3. Ability to produce board-ready reports
4. Integration with existing compliance tools
5. FUNDAE bonification availability (cost reduction)

### Language That Resonates

- "Un solo partner para ISO 27001, ENS, RGPD y NIS2"
- "Formación bonificable a través de FUNDAE"
- "Documentación integrada entre marcos normativos"
- Clear pricing and scope
- Efficiency metrics (time saved, cost reduced)

### Language That Turns Them Off

- Scope creep without transparency
- "You need everything we offer"
- Unclear pricing models
- Siloed approach to each framework

### Content Preferences

- Multi-framework comparison guides
- Cost-benefit analysis
- Implementation roadmaps
- Training program designs
- Regulatory updates and practical impact

## Persona 5: Startup Founder / CEO

**Role:** Runs the company, makes strategic decisions, often non-technical background in compliance

**Seniority:** Varies, but decision-maker for budget and priorities

### What They Care About

- **Revenue impact** — "Will ISO 27001 help me close enterprise deals?"
- **Speed** — "How fast can we get certified?"
- **Cost** — "What's the real budget, including hidden costs?"
- **Delegation** — "Can someone else handle this so I can focus on product?"
- **Credibility** — "Will this make us look more trustworthy to investors and clients?"

### What They're Skeptical About

- Compliance as a money pit with no ROI
- Long, unclear timelines
- Technical jargon they can't translate to business outcomes
- "You need 6 months and 50K" without clear deliverables

### How They Evaluate Partners

1. Can they explain the process in plain language?
2. Clear timeline and budget
3. Case studies from companies like theirs (size, stage, sector)
4. Minimal disruption to team
5. "Do it for me" capability

### Language That Resonates

- "Tus competidores ya están certificados"
- "De 0 a certificado en X meses"
- "Nos encargamos de todo para que tú te centres en tu producto"
- "Factorial, Cobee y Wallapop confiaron en nosotros"
- ROI examples and business impact stories

### Language That Turns Them Off

- Excessive legal or technical jargon
- "Depende" as the answer to every question
- Open-ended scope and pricing
- Fear-based messaging about fines

### Content Preferences

- Business-oriented blog posts (ROI, market access, competitive advantage)
- Quick guides and checklists
- Customer success stories
- "How long / how much" content
- Comparison content (what certification do I need?)

## Using Personas in Content

When writing for a specific persona:

1. **Lead with their primary concern** — What keeps them up at night?
2. **Address their skepticism directly** — Don't avoid the hard questions
3. **Use their language** — Mirror how they talk about compliance
4. **Match their format preferences** — A CEO doesn't want a 20-page whitepaper

### Cross-Persona Content

Most PrivaLex content needs to work for multiple personas. Default priority:

1. **CTO / Startup Founder** (they're the entry point — they decide to invest in compliance)
2. **CISO / Compliance Officer** (they evaluate partners and manage the project)
3. **DPO** (they're involved but rarely the initial decision-maker for certification)
